## Notes

The small default run before this pass was dominated by fixed overhead. The
larger hardware pass fixed the training and inference bands for DP, FSDP, and
decode, which makes the remaining failures more specific.

Open questions:

- Roofline: the measured crossover moved farther out, from `1024` on the small
  sweep to `2048` on the extended sweep, while throughput was still climbing at
  `8192`. The current crossover detector is probably measuring "first point at
  half of observed peak", not the real asymptote.
- Tensor parallel training: TP compiled many more layer collectives and landed
  `3.5x` slower than FSDP. The trace confirms the cause: communication rises
  from `1.4%` in FSDP to `13.1%` in TP, with all-reduces dominating the
  communication-heavy top ops. The compute-only MFU band is missing this cost
  for the tiny transformer shape.

## Resolution (2026-07-02, pending re-validation)

Both open questions were model/detector gaps, not hardware mysteries:

- **Roofline** had two compounding problems. (1) At `K=N=4096` bf16, the flat
  region's memory floor is `33.6MB / 819GB/s ~ 41us` — below TPU dispatch
  overhead, so the flat region was launch-overhead-bound (a third regime the
  two-regime napkin ignores) and the knee appeared far too late. Fix: the
  hardware pass now runs `ROOFLINE_KN=16384`, raising the floor to `~650us`.
  (2) The "half of observed peak" detector needs a saturated peak; replaced
  with the intersection of the two asymptotes (flat-region time / large-batch
  slope), plus a printed diagnosis of which regime the flat region is actually
  in (implied GB/s vs spec) and a new `peak_tflops` band receipt.
- **TP step time** was predictable all along: TP does ~4 activation
  all-reduces per layer per step. Priced with the all-reduce bandwidth
  measured in the Act 3 pass (`75GB/s` effective), that adds `~18.8ms` to this
  run's compute band, giving `[20.9, 31.6]ms` — and the measured `29.59ms` is
  in band. train.py now includes this term in the TP prediction on TPU.
